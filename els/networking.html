Using Elevator Pitches to Network Effectively

Home

What I enjoy about networking is it's open, collaborative nature. As we practiced this semester, an elevator pitch can help concisely demonstrate your interests and experiences. I've actually continued practicing and changing my elevator speech since we presented them in class because I'll be starting my full-time job in June 2016 and I'll be meeting dozens of new people in the first few weeks.

My first elevator speech was actually during Sophomore year at Tufts in my ELS 101 class. I'm thankful for the introduction to the concept because I used mine relentlessly in my 4 career fairs that I've attended. During my first career fair, I was even specifically complimented that my pitch was well-developed for an underclassmen, and I later secured an interview from that exchange. I've also used it in dedicated networking events.

Going forward, I'll need to continually revise my elevator pitch to reflect changes in my current achievements and goals. I'll continue to use it in formal and informal settings, and will perfect it with much practice.
